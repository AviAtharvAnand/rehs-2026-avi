# Master Recycling Guide

## Part 7 --- Textiles, Bulky Items & Special Collection Programs

### Overview

Many household items are not suitable for curbside recycling but can
often be reused, donated, repaired, or recycled through specialized
collection programs. Extending the life of these items reduces waste,
conserves resources, and supports the circular economy.

> **Important:** Whenever possible, reuse or donate usable items before
> considering disposal or recycling.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Textiles

## What Are Textiles?

Textiles include clothing, household fabrics, and other fabric-based
products.

### Common Examples

-   Shirts
-   Pants
-   Dresses
-   Jackets
-   Sweaters
-   Socks
-   Shoes
-   Boots
-   Belts
-   Hats
-   Scarves
-   Blankets
-   Comforters
-   Towels
-   Curtains
-   Bed sheets
-   Pillowcases
-   Fabric scraps

## Preferred Disposal Options

1.  Donate usable clothing.
2.  Sell or swap gently used items.
3.  Use textile recycling programs for worn-out fabrics.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Shoes

Many organizations accept:

-   Athletic shoes
-   Casual shoes
-   Sandals
-   Boots

Donate wearable shoes.

Recycle damaged shoes through specialty programs where available.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Furniture

Usable furniture should be:

-   Donated
-   Sold
-   Reused

Examples:

-   Chairs
-   Tables
-   Sofas
-   Dressers
-   Bookshelves
-   Mattresses (where recycling programs exist)

Many municipalities offer bulky-item pickup by appointment.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Mattresses

Many states and municipalities operate mattress recycling programs.

Components such as:

-   Steel springs
-   Foam
-   Wood
-   Fabric

can often be recovered.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Carpets & Rugs

Carpets are generally not accepted in curbside recycling.

Options include:

-   Carpet recycling facilities
-   Manufacturer take-back programs
-   Reuse when possible

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Tires

Do not place tires in curbside recycling.

Many options include:

-   Tire retailers
-   Automotive service centers
-   Community collection events

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Appliances

Large appliances often contain recyclable metals.

Examples:

-   Refrigerators
-   Freezers
-   Washing machines
-   Dryers
-   Dishwashers
-   Water heaters
-   Ovens
-   Stoves

Older appliances may require refrigerant removal before recycling.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Scrap Metal

Many recycling centers accept:

-   Bicycles
-   Metal shelving
-   Aluminum ladders
-   Metal pipes
-   Metal fencing
-   Filing cabinets

Size limits vary by facility.

# Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I recycle clothes?

Usually not in curbside recycling. Donate or use textile recycling.

### Can I recycle shoes?

Use donation or shoe recycling programs.

### Can I recycle a couch?

Schedule bulky-item collection or donate if usable.

### Can I recycle a mattress?

Use a mattress recycling program where available.

### Can I recycle a bicycle?

Many scrap metal recyclers accept bicycles.

### Can I recycle a refrigerator?

Yes through appliance recycling programs.

### Can I recycle tires?

Use tire retailers or approved collection sites.
