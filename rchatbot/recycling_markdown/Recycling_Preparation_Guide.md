# Preparing Items for Recycling

The most important step in recycling is preparing items properly so they
do not contaminate entire loads of recyclable material.

## Core Preparation Steps

-   Empty all liquid and food remnants completely.
-   Rinse jars and containers to remove thick residue such as peanut
    butter or grease.
-   Dry items thoroughly because wet paper and cardboard cannot be
    recycled.
-   Place loose items directly into the recycling bin instead of bagging
    them.
-   Remove plastic pumps, spray nozzles, and absorbent pad liners from
    meat trays.
-   Leave plastic bottle caps and lids on after flattening the main
    bottle.

## Why the Process Matters

### Prevents Contamination

-   Food residue can rot, ruin paper products, and cause entire
    truckloads of recyclables to be sent to landfills.

### Protects Machinery

-   Plastic bags and unflattened boxes can jam sorting equipment and
    shut down recycling facilities.

### Ensures Safety

-   Leftover chemicals or liquids can create hazardous reactions or
    injure waste management workers.
