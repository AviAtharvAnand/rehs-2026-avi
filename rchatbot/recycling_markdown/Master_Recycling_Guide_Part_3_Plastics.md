# Master Recycling Guide

## Part 3 --- Plastics

### Overview

Plastic is one of the most misunderstood recyclable materials. Unlike
glass or aluminum, **not every plastic item is accepted in every
recycling program**. Acceptance depends on the material type, local
equipment, and market demand.

> **Important:** The number inside the recycling symbol (#1--#7)
> identifies the plastic resin. It **does not guarantee** that an item
> is recyclable in your community.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Commonly Accepted Plastic Containers

Many curbside programs accept rigid plastic containers such as:

-   Water bottles
-   Soda bottles
-   Juice bottles
-   Milk jugs
-   Detergent bottles
-   Bleach bottles
-   Shampoo bottles
-   Conditioner bottles
-   Body wash bottles
-   Dish soap bottles
-   Yogurt tubs
-   Cottage cheese containers
-   Sour cream tubs
-   Margarine tubs
-   Peanut butter jars
-   Plastic food storage containers
-   Plastic takeout containers (clean)
-   Berry clamshell containers (where accepted)
-   Prescription bottles (where accepted)

Before recycling:

-   Empty completely.
-   Rinse away food residue.
-   Replace caps if your local program allows.
-   Keep containers loose in the cart.

# Plastic Resin Codes

## #1 PET (Polyethylene Terephthalate)

Examples: - Water bottles - Soft drink bottles - Salad dressing bottles

Widely accepted.

## #2 HDPE (High-Density Polyethylene)

Examples: - Milk jugs - Laundry detergent bottles - Shampoo bottles

Widely accepted.

## #3 PVC

Examples: - Plumbing pipe - Vinyl products

Rarely accepted curbside.

## #4 LDPE

Examples: - Bread bags - Produce bags - Plastic wrap

Usually **not** accepted curbside. Some stores offer drop-off recycling.

## #5 PP (Polypropylene)

Examples: - Yogurt tubs - Medicine bottles - Food containers

Increasingly accepted.

## #6 PS (Polystyrene)

Examples: - Foam cups - Foam takeout containers - Packing peanuts

Usually not accepted curbside.

## #7 Other

Mixed plastics and specialty materials.

Acceptance varies.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Plastic Film

Usually NOT accepted in curbside recycling:

-   Grocery bags
-   Bread bags
-   Produce bags
-   Bubble wrap
-   Stretch wrap
-   Shrink wrap
-   Dry-cleaning bags

Reason: Plastic film tangles sorting machinery.

Use retail drop-off programs where available.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Flexible Packaging

Usually not accepted:

-   Chip bags
-   Candy wrappers
-   Snack wrappers
-   Juice pouches
-   Frozen food pouches
-   Stand-up food pouches

These products often contain multiple bonded materials that are
difficult to separate.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Foam and Styrofoam®

Usually not accepted:

-   Foam cups
-   Foam plates
-   Foam egg cartons
-   Foam coolers
-   Foam takeout boxes

Some specialty recycling programs may accept clean foam.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Plastic Lids and Caps

Rules vary.

Many programs allow:

-   Bottle caps left on bottles
-   Plastic lids attached to containers

Very small loose caps may fall through sorting equipment.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Plastic Toys

Usually not accepted curbside.

Examples:

-   Action figures
-   Building blocks
-   Dolls
-   Toy cars

Donate usable toys whenever possible.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Plastic Utensils

Usually not accepted:

-   Forks
-   Spoons
-   Knives
-   Straws
-   Stir sticks

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Common Questions

### Can I recycle a water bottle?

Usually yes.

### Can I recycle a yogurt container?

Often yes.

### Can I recycle a peanut butter jar?

Usually yes after emptying and rinsing.

### Can I recycle a chip bag?

Usually no.

### Can I recycle bubble wrap?

Usually no in curbside recycling.

### Can I recycle grocery bags?

Usually no in curbside recycling. Use store drop-off bins if available.

### Can I recycle a shampoo bottle?

Usually yes.

### Can I recycle a disposable coffee lid?

Depends on the material and local program.

### Can I recycle a plastic straw?

Usually no.

### Can I recycle Styrofoam?

Most curbside programs do not accept it.

# Common Mistakes

Avoid placing these in recycling:

-   Garden hoses
-   Pool toys
-   Inflatable items
-   Plastic hangers
-   Vinyl flooring
-   Shower curtains
-   Plastic rope
-   Extension cords

# Best Practices

-   Empty containers.
-   Rinse away food.
-   Do not bag recyclables.
-   Keep plastic film out of curbside recycling.
-   Check local rules for plastics #3--#7.
