# Master Recycling Guide

## Part 2 --- Paper & Cardboard

### Overview

Paper and cardboard are among the most commonly recycled materials.
Paper fibers can be recycled several times before becoming too short for
reuse. Keeping paper **clean and dry** is the most important factor in
successful recycling.

> **Note:** Local recycling programs vary. Always follow your city's
> recycling rules if they differ from this guide.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Paper Products Commonly Accepted

The following paper products are accepted by most curbside recycling
programs:

-   Office paper
-   Printer paper
-   Copy paper
-   Notebook paper
-   Writing paper
-   Loose-leaf paper
-   White paper
-   Colored paper
-   Construction paper (where accepted)
-   Newspaper
-   Newspaper inserts
-   Magazines
-   Catalogs
-   Phone books
-   Junk mail
-   Flyers
-   Brochures
-   Envelopes (with or without windows)
-   Greeting cards (without electronics)
-   Paper grocery bags
-   Paper gift bags (without glitter or foil)
-   Paper egg cartons
-   Tissue boxes
-   Cereal boxes
-   Cracker boxes
-   Pasta boxes
-   Shoe boxes
-   Cardboard packaging

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Cardboard Commonly Accepted

Most recycling programs accept:

-   Corrugated cardboard
-   Shipping boxes
-   Online shopping boxes
-   Moving boxes
-   Appliance boxes
-   Product packaging
-   Beverage cartons (where accepted)

Before recycling:

-   Flatten boxes.
-   Remove excessive tape when practical.
-   Remove plastic liners and packing materials.
-   Keep cardboard dry.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Paper Products Usually NOT Accepted

These items are often not recyclable through curbside programs:

-   Paper towels
-   Used napkins
-   Facial tissues
-   Toilet paper
-   Wax paper
-   Wax-coated cardboard
-   Laminated paper
-   Carbon paper
-   Thermal receipt paper (varies)
-   Glitter paper
-   Metallic wrapping paper
-   Plastic-coated paper
-   Disposable paper plates with food residue

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Pizza Boxes

## Can I recycle a pizza box?

Usually:

-   Clean portions can be recycled.
-   Greasy portions should be composted or discarded according to local
    guidance.

If the entire box is soaked with grease, it generally should not be
recycled.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Shredded Paper

Shredded paper is accepted by some programs but not others.

Best practices:

-   Place shredded paper in a paper bag if your program requests it.
-   Never allow loose shredded paper to blow away.

Examples:

-   Bank statements
-   Personal documents
-   School papers

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Paper Bags

Paper grocery bags are usually recyclable if:

-   Clean
-   Dry
-   Free of food residue

Do not recycle:

-   Greasy paper bags
-   Wet paper bags

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Gift Wrap

Usually recyclable:

-   Plain paper gift wrap

Usually not recyclable:

-   Foil gift wrap
-   Glitter gift wrap
-   Plastic-coated gift wrap
-   Ribbon
-   Bows

# Common Questions

### Can I recycle receipts?

Thermal receipts are often not recyclable because of their chemical
coating.

### Can I recycle books?

Hardcover books usually require removal of the cover. Paperback books
are accepted in many communities.

### Can I recycle sticky notes?

Generally yes, in small quantities.

### Can I recycle envelopes with plastic windows?

Usually yes.

### Can I recycle cereal boxes?

Yes.

### Can I recycle milk cartons?

Many communities accept cartons. Check local guidance.

### Can I recycle paper cups?

Disposable coffee cups often contain a plastic lining and are usually
not recyclable.

### Can I recycle paper towels?

Usually no.

### Can I recycle tissues?

Usually no.

### Can I recycle newspapers?

Yes.

### Can I recycle magazines?

Yes.

### Can I recycle cardboard tubes?

Often yes if clean.

### Can I recycle greeting cards?

Yes, unless they contain batteries, electronics, or heavy decorations.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Common Mistakes

Avoid placing these in paper recycling:

-   Wet cardboard
-   Food-soiled paper
-   Plastic wrapping
-   Bubble mailers
-   Padded envelopes
-   Wax-coated cartons
-   Plastic shipping envelopes

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Preparation Checklist

Before recycling paper or cardboard:

-   Remove food.
-   Keep materials dry.
-   Flatten boxes.
-   Remove plastic packing.
-   Do not bag paper recyclables unless instructed.
