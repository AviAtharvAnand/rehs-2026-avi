# Master Recycling Guide

## Part 6 --- Electronics, Batteries & Household Hazardous Waste

### Overview

Electronics, batteries, and household hazardous waste (HHW) require
special handling. These items often contain valuable materials that can
be recovered, but they may also contain hazardous substances that can
harm people and the environment if placed in curbside recycling or the
trash.

> **Important:** Never place batteries, electronics, or hazardous
> chemicals in a curbside recycling bin unless your local program
> specifically instructs you to do so.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Electronics (E-Waste)

Electronic devices contain metals, plastics, and circuit boards that
should be recycled through certified e-waste programs.

## Common Examples

-   Desktop computers
-   Laptops
-   Tablets
-   Mobile phones
-   Monitors
-   Televisions
-   Printers
-   Scanners
-   Keyboards
-   Computer mice
-   Routers
-   Modems
-   Video game consoles
-   DVD and Blu-ray players
-   Digital cameras
-   Smart speakers
-   Small electronic toys

## Best Practices

-   Back up and erase personal data before recycling.
-   Remove batteries when practical.
-   Take electronics to certified e-waste collection sites.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Batteries

## Never Place in Curbside Recycling

Loose batteries can short-circuit and start fires during collection or
sorting.

## Common Battery Types

-   AA
-   AAA
-   C
-   D
-   9-volt
-   Button batteries
-   Lithium-ion batteries
-   Rechargeable batteries
-   Power tool batteries
-   Laptop batteries
-   Cell phone batteries
-   Car batteries

## Safe Handling

-   Tape the terminals of lithium-ion and 9-volt batteries before
    transporting them.
-   Store damaged batteries separately in a nonflammable container.
-   Use designated battery collection locations.

# Household Hazardous Waste (HHW)

Household hazardous waste includes products that are flammable,
corrosive, reactive, or toxic.

## Common Examples

-   Paint
-   Paint thinner
-   Motor oil
-   Oil filters
-   Antifreeze
-   Gasoline
-   Pesticides
-   Herbicides
-   Pool chemicals
-   Cleaning chemicals
-   Solvents
-   Mercury thermometers
-   Fluorescent tubes
-   CFL bulbs

These items should be taken to an approved HHW collection facility.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Light Bulbs

## Usually Accepted Through Special Collection

-   CFL bulbs
-   Fluorescent tubes
-   HID bulbs

## Often Allowed in Trash (Check Local Rules)

-   Incandescent bulbs
-   Halogen bulbs

Never place mercury-containing bulbs in curbside recycling.

# Smoke & Carbon Monoxide Detectors

Smoke detectors may contain radioactive materials or specialized
electronics.

Follow manufacturer or local government disposal instructions.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Ink & Toner Cartridges

Many retailers and manufacturers offer take-back programs for:

-   Ink cartridges
-   Toner cartridges

Reuse or refill when possible.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Medicines

Do not place unused medicines in curbside recycling.

Use:

-   Pharmacy take-back programs
-   Community drug take-back events
-   Permanent medication collection kiosks

Follow local guidance before flushing medications.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Sharps & Medical Waste

Examples:

-   Needles
-   Syringes
-   Lancets

Never place loose sharps in recycling or regular trash.

Use approved sharps containers and community disposal programs.

# Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I recycle a laptop?

Not in curbside recycling. Use an e-waste recycler.

### Can I recycle a TV?

Use an approved electronics recycling program.

### Can I recycle rechargeable batteries?

No. Use battery collection programs.

### Can I recycle alkaline batteries?

Rules vary by state and locality. Follow local guidance.

### Can I recycle fluorescent light bulbs?

Use a household hazardous waste or retailer collection program.

### Can I recycle motor oil?

Motor oil is commonly accepted through automotive recycling or HHW
programs.

### Can I recycle paint?

Latex paint disposal varies by state. Oil-based paint is typically HHW.
